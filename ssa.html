The term "SSY" typically refers to the Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana, a savings scheme in India aimed at promoting the financial security of girls. The application procedure for the Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY) may involve both online and offline methods. Please note that procedures and requirements may change over time, so it's essential to check the latest guidelines from the relevant authority. Here are the general steps for applying online for SSY as of my last update:

1.Eligibility Check:

  • VEnsure that you meet the eligibility criteria for opening an SSY account, which generally involves having a female child below a certain age.

    • Visit the Official Bank or Post Office Website:

  • SSY accounts can typically be opened through authorized banks and post offices in India. Visit the official website of the bank or post office where you want to open the SSY account.

    • Download the SSY Account Opening Form:

  • Look for the Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana account opening form on the bank or post office's website. Download and print the form.

    • Fill in the Form:

  • Fill out the SSY account opening form with accurate and complete details. You will need to provide information about the child and the guardian/parent opening the account.

    • Gather Required Documents:

  • Collect the necessary documents, which typically include the child's birth certificate, proof of identity, proof of residence, and a passport-sized photograph.

    • Visit the Bank or Post Office:

  • Visit the selected bank or post office branch in person with the filled-in form and supporting documents

    • Submit the Application:

  • Submit the SSY account opening form along with the required documents to the bank/post office personnel.

    • Deposit the Initial Amount:

  • You will need to make an initial deposit into the SSY account. The minimum deposit amount may vary, so check with the specific bank or post office.

    • Receive Passbook and Account Details:

  • After the application is processed, you will be provided with a passbook for the SSY account, which will contain all the account details.

    • Keep Track of the Account:

  • Regularly monitor the account and make subsequent deposits as required.

    • Please remember that the Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana is subject to government rules and regulations, and it's crucial to verify the most up-to-date information and procedures from the official sources, such as the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) or the specific bank or post office where you plan to open the account. Additionally, the procedure may vary depending on any changes in government policies or financial institutions' processes.
